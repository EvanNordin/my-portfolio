About me

Information

My name is Evan Nordin. I have been a software developer for the last seven years and began studying mathematics to become a data scientist three years ago. My love for software engineering, web development, and data science all stems from my passion for solving problems. I am excellent at independently completing tasks I am given, but I really thrive in collaborative environments where I am given the opportunity to share my knowledge and learn from others. I am very detail-oriented, which is why I really enjoy building software. A few of my favorite books that I've recently read include "The Pragmatic Programmer", "Designing Data-Intensive Applications", and "Eloquent Javascript". While there are many more books I have read and learned from, I feel that each of these books has influenced the way I approach and solve problems. I never get tired of looking back over them. I am familar with numerous SDLC methods, but my favorite is AGILE.

My Timeline

2017 - June 2022

Bachelor of Science: Computer Science - Central Washington University

Studied Computer Science at Central Washington University. During my time at CWU I learned extensivesly about the fundamentals of computer science, including data structures, algorithms, cybersecurity, computer architecture, principles of language design, database management systems, algorithm analysis, advanced software engineering, and most recently computational intelligence (AI and ML algorithms).

2021 - Present

Technology Intern - CBHA

Currently working as a technology intern at Columbia Basin Health Association (CBHA). As a tech intern, I have played a significant role in the implementation of the KACE asset management system. At the beginning of my internship, I was responsible for physically tagging over two thousand assets with barcodes and putting them in the new asset management system. After this project was complete, I developed a custom web application that allows CBHA to send out information system alerts to its employees via text, and also as a popup on the employee's computer. I am currently building an application that streamlines the processs of updating asset information inside of KACE.

Studied Applied Mathematics at Central Washington University. During my time at CWU I learned extensivesly about the fundamentals of applied mathematics, including linear algebra, calculus, probability and statistics, differential equations, cryptography, mathematical modeling, and most recently advanced numerical methods and analysis.
